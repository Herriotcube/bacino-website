Authentic Sicilian Cannoli Recipe with Ricotta Filling

Introduction Have you ever tried making an Authentic Sicilian Cannoli Recipe with Ricotta Filling? If not, you’re in for a treat! Cannoli is an iconic Sicilian dessert featuring crispy, golden-brown shells stuffed with luscious sweetened ricotta cream. This delicacy isn’t just a dessert; it’s a bite of history. Why is it so special?

Here are a few reasons why cannoli is such a special dessert: Significance and Popularity: These treats have transcended borders, becoming beloved worldwide. Each bite offers a delightful crunch followed by the creamy richness of ricotta – it’s no wonder they’re so popular! Rich Culinary History: Originating from Sicily, cannoli were initially made during Carnevale, an Italian festival held before Lent.

The name “cannolo” means “little tube” in Italian, referring to the shape of the fried pastry shell. Over centuries, this festive dessert has evolved yet retained its authentic charm. Ready to embark on this culinary journey? Let’s dive deep into creating these heavenly delights at home!

Ingredients and Instructions Cannoli Shells Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour 1 tablespoon granulated sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 egg, beaten 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted 1/2 cup Marsala wine (or milk as an alternative) Vegetable oil for frying Instructions: Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.

Add Wet Ingredients: Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and add the beaten egg, melted butter, and Marsala wine. Mix until a dough forms. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Rest the Dough: Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

Roll Out the Dough: Divide the dough into quarters and roll each piece out to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut and Shape: Use a round cutter (about 4 inches in diameter) to cut circles from the dough. Wrap each circle around a metal cannoli tube, sealing the edges with a bit of beaten egg. Fry to Perfection: Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F (190°C).

Fry the dough-wrapped tubes in batches until golden brown and bubbly, approximately 2-3 minutes per side. Cool and Remove Tubes: Carefully remove shells from oil and drain on paper towels. Once cool enough to handle, gently slide them off the tubes.

Ricotta Filling Ingredients: 2 cups fresh ricotta cheese (preferably sheep’s milk ricotta) 1 cup powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Optional: 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips or candied orange peel Instructions: Drain Ricotta: Place ricotta cheese in a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth-lined colander to drain excess moisture for at least one hour.

Mix Filling Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine drained ricotta cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. Add Extras (if desired): Fold in dark chocolate chips or candied orange peel for added texture and flavor. This sets you up perfectly for crafting those crispy shells and luscious fillings that make Sicilian Cannoli so irresistible! Ready to bring these elements together?

Let’s dive into filling, decorating, and serving these delightful treats next! Filling, Decorating, and Serving the Cannoli Filling the Cannoli Shells Let’s get those gorgeous shells filled with that dreamy ricotta cream. Here’s how: Pastry Bag Method: This is the go-to method for many. Fill a pastry bag fitted with a large round tip or just snip off the end of a freezer bag.

Pipe the ricotta filling into each shell from both ends to ensure they are fully stuffed. Spoon Method: If you don’t have a pastry bag, no worries! Use a small spoon to carefully fill each shell. Hold one end of the shell up and scoop in the filling, pushing it gently to avoid breaking the delicate shell. Decorating Your Cannoli Time to jazz up those bad boys!

Adding some extra flair to your cannoli not only makes them look stunning but adds an extra layer of flavor and texture. Chocolate Chips: Dip the ends of your filled cannoli into mini chocolate chips. This gives a delightful crunch and that chocolatey goodness we all crave. Pistachios: Finely chopped pistachios add a pop of color and a nutty twist. Just press them onto the ends after filling.

Melted Chocolate Drizzle: Go artistic by drizzling melted chocolate over the tops. Use dark, milk, or even white chocolate – whatever floats your boat! Serving These Delectable Treats You’ve worked hard, now it’s time to enjoy these beauties! Timing is Key: Serve your filled cannoli as soon as possible. They’re best enjoyed within 15 minutes of filling to keep that perfect crispiness.

Refrigeration: If you need to wait before serving, you can refrigerate them for up to 2 hours but any longer might soften those crispy shells. Presentation: Place your decorated cannoli on a nice platter. Sprinkle some powdered sugar on top for that final touch. Watch everyone’s eyes light up!

Troubleshooting Common Issues with Sicilian Cannoli Making Sicilian Cannoli at home can be super rewarding, but sometimes things don’t go as planned. Here’s your handy guide to troubleshooting those pesky problems. Common Problems and Solutions Soggy Shells Biting into a cannolo only to find it’s gone soggy is a major bummer. Solution: Timing is Key: Fill the shells just before serving.

Once filled, they should be eaten within 15 minutes for the best crunch. Proper Frying: Ensure the oil is hot enough (around 375°F/190°C). If it’s not, the shells will absorb too much oil and turn out greasy. Storage: Store unfilled shells in an airtight container to maintain their crispiness. Runny Filling A runny filling can turn your cannoli dreams into a nightmare.

Solution: Drain the Ricotta: Place ricotta in a cheesecloth-lined strainer and let it drain overnight in the refrigerator. This helps remove excess moisture. Right Consistency: Mix in powdered sugar gradually until you achieve a thick, creamy consistency. Avoid using granulated sugar, which can make the mixture watery.

Chill Time: Let the filling chill in the fridge for at least an hour before piping it into the shells. Additional Tips for Perfection Shell Thickness: Roll out the dough thinly but not too thin. About 1/8 inch thickness works well. Thinner dough ensures crispy shells without being overly delicate. Oil Temperature: Use a thermometer to monitor oil temperature.

Too hot and your shells will burn; too cool and they’ll turn out greasy and soggy. Sealing Shells: When forming your tubes, seal the edges with egg white to prevent them from opening up during frying. Achieving Bakery-Quality Results Consistency and attention to detail are key. Practice makes perfect when it comes to cannoli-making. Don’t get discouraged if your first batch isn’t picture-perfect.

Each attempt brings you closer to mastering this delicious Sicilian dessert. Experiment with Variations like adding chocolate chips or candied fruit to your filling once you’ve nailed down the basics.
